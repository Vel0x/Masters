\abstract{
With the increasing importance of the issue of the adverse effects air pollution has on citizens' health, air quality measurements in urban environments are on the rise. In this study we look at the performance of using buses in the city of Edinburgh as a mobility mechanism for air quality sensors. Specifically, through the means of simulation, we evaluate the impact of various parameter changes in a model, discovering that using opportunistic forwarding provides the greatest performance improvement. We then use data similar to that generated by such a system to evaluate the effectiveness of various simple spatial interpolation methods on urban air quality data sets. We find that Barnes interpolation gives the best performance out of the algorithms tested, but not with an accuracy great enough to be used for any formal scientific context.
}
